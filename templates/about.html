What's this About

What is a Superconductor?

A superconductor is a material which can conduct electricity without any resistance. The material becomes "superconducting" only below a certain temperature — Critical Temperature.
Read more: Wikipedia

The Challenge:

Finding a material that becomes superconducting at room temperatures.

Who will benefit from it?

  • Energy Industry:

    Efficient energy transmission.

  • Quantum Computing:

    Reducing operational cost for Quantum computers.

  • Transportation:

    MagLev

  • Researchers:

    Those who leverage the novel properties of superconductors for exploring and discovering novelties of other materials.

How to use this site?

  • Given a set of Elements:

    Select the elements in the page.
    Find the optimal composition which maximizes Critical Temperature.
    Bonus: Cross check the Max(Tc) of this composition from page.

  • If you know the Composition:

    Select the relative proportion of elements in the page.
    Get the Critical Temperature.
    Bonus: Also checks for nearby compositions for a larger Critical Temperature.
